Interactive Media Assignment 1

Interactive Experience Research Project by Kelly Xiao (s4002452)


Chosen website: MyoungAe Kim's Web portfolio

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to was the visual design of the website. The monochromatic aesthetic paired with the minimalistic layout made it look easy to navigate and explore. I paid a lot of attention to the interactive calendar boxes on the left, as the concept was interesting and interacting with the Christmas objects hidden behind them was fun to play with.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

•   Clicking on all the interactive boxes on the left

•   Exploring and moving the Christmas objects around

•   Clicking on “Merry Christmas” in box 5, which turned the background colour of website red.

•   Scrolling down and reading the projects/portfolioon the right

•   Click into the title of one of them to see the images better

•   Going through all of them through the navigational links below the portfolio description

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The part of the experience I spent the most time engaging with was the interactive boxes on the left. Reading paragraphs of MyoungAe Kim’s portfolio work was interesting, but not as fun as clicking the boxes and interacting with the Christmas objects hidden inside. Through engaging with the interactive calendar boxes, I also discovered the website background colour could be changed to red by clicking on, “Merry Christmas” hidden in the 5th box.

4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action in my two-minute interaction with the website was interacting with the calendar boxes. I clicked a lot on those boxes for a solid minute and dragged the hidden Christmas objects around with my mouse. I also spent some time clicking through the navigation links to the pages of her project works, scrolling up and down the images quickly to get an overview of her portfolio.

5. What is the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

MyoungAe Kim’s primary goal for this interactive experience was to not only celebrate the year-end Christmas but also to showcase her portfolio work and style. She achieves this by designing this website with an advent calendar theme and a simple monochrome layout to highlight her minimalistic style.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The main goal of the website was to celebrate the year-end, while also showcasing MyoungAe Kim’s portfolio. The website shows elements of fun surprises through hidden interactive Christmas objects and a background change within opening the calendar boxes. She effectively showcases her portfolio work through simple navigation, clear project images, bolded titles, and nicely spaced text, while keeping a minimalistic style that matches her work.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

My impression of this website is that it’s designed for casual visitors, students, employers, etc. For casual visitors and students looking at the web portfolio for educational purposes, I think their experience would be more about exploring and taking their time interacting with the website and calendar boxes, which could last moderately 10-30 mins. For employers, they may take multiple visits with short minute durations to quickly peek at some of the work the designer has done for reference.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with over time through the simple layout and clear visual cues that guides user interaction and easy navigation. For example, the titles of her portfolio works are bolded and underlined, indicating to the user that it can be clicked on for further information. Additionally, the Christmas objects shake when the user hovers the mouse on it, which is another visual cue that users can interact with it and move it around. Overtime, these design patterns and simple layout helps users intuitively understand how to interact with the website.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This website experience references other media forms, such as an advent calendar. Advent calendars feature small, numbered flaps, each of which is opened daily during Advent to reveal a seasonally themed image. The numbered boxes in the website is similar to this, as some of the boxes have hidden Christmas themed objects that are interactable. Furthermore, the website’s minimalistic monochrome layout paired with small black circles in the corner of the portfolio work pages also reminds me of a notebook.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The interactive boxes on the website, reminiscent of an Advent calendar, guide user behaviour by encouraging curiosity and exploration. Just how an Advent calendar invites users to uncover a new surprise each day, these interactive elements invite me to explore by clicking on the boxes to open them.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references in this website communicate that I should feel a mix of professionalism and festive joy when interacting with the research experience. The simple monochromatic design and portfolio images carry a modern and professional feel, while the interactive boxes bring elements of surprise and enjoyment as I explore the hidden festive objects.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

In my experience of interacting with this website, there are a few frustrating parts. If I had to nitpick, the tiny numbers under the images that allow the user to navigate through more images are too small to be noticeable and it is annoying to click on each number for each image. Another frustrating part is when I’m looking at the description, but some of it is cut off because my screen is not full screen (on mac) and there is no scroll feature. To see the whole description, I need to make my window full screen, which is kind of frustrating. Additionally, the red background change not staying red when navigating to another page disappointed me.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction for me was the interactive calendar boxes. It was satisfying opening them for the first time and seeing Christmas-themed objects hidden inside. I also found the hidden background colour change link and found it fun to switch the colour to red and white back and forth.
